TEDxUChicago 2012 Team:

Pedro Sánchez de Lozada

is a half-Chilean half-Bolivian student at the University of Chicago and a Co-Director of TEDxUChicago. From a very young age, Pedro's uses his passion for innovation to encourage his peers to integrate technology into their daily lives and teach them about the latest and the greatest. Pedro also enjoys challenging the people around him to confront tradition and depart from their comfort zone in order to discover new ideas and develop their persona. Consequently, he has always been a devoted follower of "Ideas Worth Sharing." Among his passions are entrepreneurship, extreme sports, dance, and his collection of ceramic turtles.

Tiffany Sommadossi

is a fourth year majoring in International Studies and minoring in Human Rights at the University of Chicago, and a Co-Director of TEDxUChicago. Her internships with a political campaign, a patent law firm, and a pro bono asylum project reflect her interest in international law and policy-making. She is passionate about discovering remarkable people and learning what impacts they’re making on the world. As someone who lives her life hopping from one adventure to the next, it’s not unusual to find her doing things like wandering Haiti speaking to local artists, unable to not travel somewhere at least once a month, teaching English in Jordan, or, in the near future running an ultramarathon.

Luiz Filipe Serravite

is a third year at the College. He is studying Economics and Political Sciences and is the Co-chair of Sponsors at TEDxUChicago. He first learned about TED watching a video of “Sixth-Sense” a device invented by an MIT's Post-Doctorate student. The interest in TED conferences grew bigger and he is delighted to be organizing TEDxUChicago!

Luiz is an international student from Brazil and is a scholar from Fundacao Estudar since 2008. He has always enjoyed been involved in campus campus activities, ranging from the investment Club, The Blue Chips, to the Model United Nations team, passing through Kairos and the Brazilian Students Association, of which he is the vice-president. He is organizing another conference for next year as well on the future of the BRIC countries. During the past summers he has worked for Bain & Company in Sao Paulo, ABInBev and the American Chamber of Commerce in Belo Horizonte, his hometown.

In addition to being a car and motorsports aficionado, Luiz tries to follow the maxim “work hard, play hard” and thus enjoys spending time with friends, going out, house music and traveling, He also loves debating politics, economics and global affairs.
